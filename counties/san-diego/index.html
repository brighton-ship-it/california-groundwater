Understanding Fractured Rock Wells

Unlike sedimentary basins where water is stored in sand and gravel, much of San Diego County overlies crystalline bedrock (granite and other igneous/metamorphic rocks). Water is found in:

  • Fractures and fissures: Cracks in the rock that store and transmit water
  • Weathered zones: Decomposed rock near the surface
  • Fault zones: Often more productive due to increased fracturing

What This Means for Well Drilling

  • Well yields are less predictable than in alluvial areas
  • Neighboring properties may have very different results
  • Site-specific geological assessment is valuable
  • Hydrofracturing may improve low-yield wells

Drilling Costs

San Diego County well costs vary by area and rock type:

  • 200-foot well (coastal/alluvial): $10,000 - $18,000
  • 300-foot well (typical): $15,000 - $25,000
  • 500-foot well (deep): $25,000 - $40,000
  • Pump installation: $2,500 - $6,000 additional

Note: Drilling in hard rock is slower and more expensive per foot than in softer formations.

Water Quality

Common water quality characteristics in San Diego County wells:

  • Hard water: Very common, 15-40 gpg typical
  • High TDS: Elevated minerals in many areas
  • Fluoride: Naturally elevated in some areas
  • Iron/manganese: Common in some fractured rock zones

Test all new wells before use. Water treatment systems can address most quality issues. For treatment recommendations, consult a well water specialist.
