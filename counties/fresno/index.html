Why Wells Are Getting Deeper

Fresno County is part of the San Joaquin Valley groundwater basin, which has been classified as "critically overdrafted" under California's Sustainable Groundwater Management Act (SGMA). Key factors:

  • Agricultural pumping: Irrigation uses 80%+ of groundwater
  • Drought years: Less surface water means more groundwater use
  • Limited recharge: Less rain percolating to aquifers
  • Population growth: Increasing domestic demand

What This Means for Well Owners

  • Plan for deeper wells than historical averages suggest
  • Budget for potential future deepening
  • Monitor your water level annually
  • Consider water storage for low-yield periods

Drilling Costs

Due to deeper required depths, Fresno County well costs are higher than many California counties:

  • 400-foot domestic well: $22,000 - $35,000
  • 600-foot domestic well: $35,000 - $50,000
  • 800-foot domestic well: $50,000 - $70,000
  • Pump installation: $4,000 - $12,000 additional (deep pumps cost more)

Water Quality Considerations

Common water quality issues in Fresno County:

  • Nitrates: Very common in agricultural areas‚Äîtest before drinking
  • Arsenic: Naturally occurring in some deeper aquifers
  • Hard water: Common, 20-40 gpg in many areas
  • Salinity: Increasing in some western areas

Always test new wells for bacteria, nitrates, and minerals. Consider treatment systems if levels are elevated. Consult a well water specialist for treatment options.

SGMA and Future Groundwater Management

Under SGMA, Fresno County groundwater sustainability agencies must bring the basin into balance by 2040. This may affect:

  • New well permits (some areas restricting new ag wells)
  • Pumping allocations
  • Fees on groundwater extraction
  • Requirements to track and report usage

Check with your local Groundwater Sustainability Agency (GSA) before planning a new well.
