Understanding Your Well Water Test Results

Updated January 2025 ‚Ä¢ 15 min read

You got your water test results back. Now what? Water quality reports can be confusing with technical terms and units of measurement. This guide explains what each test means, what levels are safe, and what to do if your results show problems.

The Essential Tests Every California Well Should Have

California recommends these tests for private wells:

Test How Often Why It Matters
Total Coliform Bacteria Annually Indicates possible contamination from surface water or sewage
E. coli Annually Indicates fecal contamination‚Äîserious health risk
Nitrate Annually Agricultural runoff, septic systems‚Äîdangerous for infants
Full Mineral Panel Every 3-5 years Arsenic, iron, hardness, pH, TDS, and more

Bacteria Tests: Coliform and E. coli

What the Results Mean

Result Meaning Action
Total Coliform: Absent/Negative No bacteria detected ‚úì Safe to drink
Total Coliform: Present Bacteria found (not necessarily harmful) Retest; if confirmed, disinfect well
E. coli: Absent/Negative No fecal contamination ‚úì Safe to drink
E. coli: Present Fecal contamination detected ‚ö†Ô∏è DO NOT DRINK - Disinfect immediately

‚ö†Ô∏è If E. coli is detected:

  1. Stop drinking the water immediately
  2. Use bottled water or boil water for 1 minute before use
  3. Shock chlorinate (disinfect) the well
  4. Find and fix the contamination source
  5. Retest after disinfection

Common Causes of Bacteria

  • Damaged or missing well cap
  • Cracked well casing
  • Nearby septic system failure
  • Surface water entering the well
  • Recent well work or pump replacement

Nitrate (NO‚ÇÉ)

Nitrate is one of the most common contaminants in California wells, especially in agricultural areas.

Result (mg/L) Status Action
0-5 Good ‚úì Safe for everyone
5-10 Elevated Monitor; safe for adults, watch for infants
>10 Exceeds MCL ‚ö†Ô∏è Do not give to infants; consider treatment

Blue Baby Syndrome: Nitrate above 10 mg/L can cause methemoglobinemia in infants under 6 months. Boiling water makes nitrate concentration WORSE, not better. Use bottled water for infant formula if nitrates are high.

Reducing Nitrate

  • Reverse osmosis: Most effective treatment (90%+ removal)
  • Ion exchange: Effective but requires maintenance
  • Deeper well: Sometimes deeper aquifers have lower nitrate
  • Blending: Mix with nitrate-free water source

Arsenic

Arsenic occurs naturally in California groundwater, particularly in the foothills, Central Valley, and some coastal areas.

Result (¬µg/L or ppb) Status Action
0-5 Good ‚úì Safe
5-10 Elevated Consider treatment; monitor health
>10 Exceeds MCL ‚ö†Ô∏è Treatment recommended

Note: The EPA Maximum Contaminant Level (MCL) is 10 ¬µg/L, but some health agencies recommend staying below 5 ¬µg/L for long-term exposure.

Arsenic Treatment Options

  • Reverse osmosis: Point-of-use systems for drinking water
  • Adsorption media: Iron-based filters that bind arsenic
  • Whole-house systems: More expensive but treats all water

Iron and Manganese

These minerals are common in California wells. They're not health hazards at typical levels but cause aesthetic problems.

Contaminant Secondary MCL Problems Caused
Iron 0.3 mg/L Orange/brown staining, metallic taste, stained laundry
Manganese 0.05 mg/L Black staining, bitter taste

Treatment Options

  • Oxidation + filtration: Air injection or chlorination followed by filter
  • Water softener: Removes low levels of iron
  • Greensand filter: Effective for combined iron/manganese
  • Birm filter: Chemical-free oxidation for lower iron levels

Hardness

Hard water contains high levels of calcium and magnesium. It's not a health concern but affects appliances and cleaning.

Hardness (mg/L or ppm) Classification Effects
0-60 Soft Ideal for most uses
61-120 Moderately hard Minor scaling possible
121-180 Hard Noticeable scaling, reduced soap efficiency
>180 Very hard Significant scaling, appliance damage

Hardness in grains per gallon (gpg): Divide mg/L by 17.1. Most water softeners are sized by gpg.

pH Level

pH measures how acidic or alkaline your water is.

pH Level Classification Concerns
<6.5 Acidic Corrodes pipes, can leach metals (copper, lead)
6.5-8.5 Normal range ‚úì Ideal for most uses
>8.5 Alkaline Scale formation, bitter taste

Low pH + copper pipes = health risk. Acidic water can leach copper from pipes, causing elevated copper levels at the tap. If your pH is below 6.5 and you have copper plumbing, test for copper at the tap after water sits in pipes overnight.

Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)

TDS measures all dissolved minerals combined. It's an indicator of overall water quality.

TDS (mg/L) Classification Notes
<300 Excellent Low mineral content
300-500 Good Acceptable for most uses
500-1000 Fair May have taste; secondary MCL is 500
>1000 Poor Noticeable taste, potential scale issues

Other Common Tests

Fluoride

Natural fluoride in groundwater varies widely. MCL is 4 mg/L, but levels above 2 mg/L may cause dental fluorosis in children.

Sulfate

Can cause laxative effects above 250 mg/L. Often associated with "rotten egg" smell when combined with bacteria.

Chloride

Salty taste above 250 mg/L. High levels may indicate saltwater intrusion (coastal areas) or contamination.

Uranium

Naturally occurring in some California groundwater, especially in granite areas. MCL is 30 ¬µg/L.

Where to Get Your Water Tested

For reliable results, use a certified laboratory:

  1. County health department: Many offer free or low-cost bacteria testing
  2. State-certified labs: Search the ELAP database
  3. Mail-order labs: Convenient but verify they're certified

Testing Costs

  • Bacteria only: $20-$50
  • Bacteria + nitrate: $30-$75
  • Basic mineral panel: $75-$150
  • Comprehensive panel: $150-$400

Next Steps After Testing

Based on your results:

  1. All clear: Test again in one year (bacteria/nitrate) and 3-5 years (full panel)
  2. Minor issues: Consider point-of-use treatment (drinking water only)
  3. Significant contamination: Consult a water treatment professional for whole-house options
  4. Bacterial contamination: Disinfect well and identify contamination source

Keep your test results on file. Tracking changes over time helps identify problems early and provides documentation if you ever sell the property.
