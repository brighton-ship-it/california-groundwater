Seasonal Well Maintenance Guide for California

Updated January 2025 ‚Ä¢ 8 min read

California's climate presents unique challenges for well owners‚Äîfrom extended droughts to intense rainy seasons and occasional freezes. This season-by-season guide helps you keep your well system running reliably year-round.

Why Seasonal Maintenance Matters

Regular maintenance prevents costly emergencies and extends the life of your well system:

  • Prevent pump failure: Catching problems early avoids expensive emergency replacements
  • Protect water quality: Maintaining the wellhead prevents contamination
  • Save money: Well-maintained systems use less electricity
  • Extend equipment life: Annual care can add 5+ years to your pump's lifespan

üå± Spring (March - May)

Focus: Post-winter inspection and water testing

  • Inspect wellhead: Check for cracks, damage, or pest entry from winter
  • Test water quality: Annual bacteria and nitrate test (do this before heavy irrigation season)
  • Check well cap seal: Ensure gasket is intact and cap is secure
  • Inspect electrical connections: Look for corrosion or loose wires at pressure switch and well
  • Test pressure tank: Check air charge with tire gauge (should be 2 PSI below cut-in pressure)
  • Change sediment filters: Replace any whole-house filters
  • Clear vegetation: Remove plants growing within 10 feet of wellhead

Spring Tip: This is the best time for water testing‚Äîafter winter rains have recharged the aquifer but before summer irrigation increases demand on groundwater.

‚òÄÔ∏è Summer (June - August)

Focus: High-demand preparation and drought monitoring

  • Monitor water pressure: Note any decline as summer progresses
  • Check for air sputtering: Sign of dropping water table
  • Inspect irrigation system: Leaks waste water and overwork the pump
  • Listen for pump cycling: Frequent on/off indicates problems
  • Check water heater anode: High mineral content accelerates corrosion
  • Document baseline: Record normal operating pressure and flow rate

Drought Alert: During California's dry summers, watch for:

  • Air bubbles in water (pump near water level)
  • Gradual pressure drop
  • Pump running longer than usual
  • Well "running dry" during heavy use

If you notice these signs, reduce water usage and consider having your water level measured.

Managing High-Demand Periods

  • Stagger irrigation schedules (don't run everything at once)
  • Water lawns in early morning when evaporation is lowest
  • Let the well recover between heavy-use periods
  • Consider a storage tank for low-yield wells

üçÇ Fall (September - November)

Focus: Winterization prep and system evaluation

  • Schedule professional inspection: Best time for annual well checkup
  • Drain and store irrigation: Disconnect hoses and blow out irrigation lines
  • Insulate exposed pipes: Any pipes above ground or in unheated spaces
  • Check pressure tank bladder: Before cold weather affects readings
  • Inspect well house/pit: Ensure insulation and heating elements work
  • Service water softener: Clean brine tank and check salt level
  • Clear drainage: Ensure water drains away from wellhead before rains

Fall Tip: If you noticed any issues during summer's high-demand period, fall is the time to address them‚Äîbefore winter weather and before next summer's demand.

‚ùÑÔ∏è Winter (December - February)

Focus: Freeze protection and wet-weather contamination prevention

  • Protect from freezing: Insulate pipes, pressure tank, and exposed components
  • Check well cap after storms: Heavy rain can shift or damage caps
  • Monitor for flooding: Don't let floodwater reach wellhead
  • Inspect after power outages: Check that pump restarts properly
  • Keep access clear: Ensure you can reach well in emergency

Freeze Protection for California

While California is mild, freezing can occur in:

  • Mountain and foothill areas
  • High desert regions
  • Northern California valleys (radiation freezes)

Freeze protection options:

  • Insulation wrap on exposed pipes
  • Heat tape for vulnerable areas
  • Insulated well house or pump cover
  • Allow a small trickle during freeze warnings

After Flooding: If floodwater reached your wellhead, have the water tested for bacteria before drinking. The well may need shock chlorination.

Annual Professional Inspection

Beyond DIY maintenance, schedule a professional inspection every 3-5 years (annually for older wells). A pro can:

  • Measure static and pumping water levels
  • Test pump flow rate and efficiency
  • Camera inspect the well bore
  • Check casing integrity
  • Evaluate pump condition
  • Identify developing problems

Cost: $150-$400 for a basic inspection. Well camera inspection costs $200-$500 extra but provides valuable information about well condition.

Maintenance Record Keeping

Keep a log of:

  • Water test results: Date and lab, all parameters tested
  • System measurements: Pressure readings, flow rate tests
  • Maintenance performed: Filter changes, inspections, repairs
  • Professional service: Who, when, what was done
  • Problems observed: Any changes in water quality or quantity

This record helps diagnose problems, proves maintenance history for property sales, and helps contractors understand your system.

Quick Reference: Monthly Checks

Takes just 5 minutes:

  • Listen to pump operation‚Äîany unusual sounds?
  • Check water pressure at a faucet
  • Look at water clarity and taste
  • Glance at wellhead for obvious problems
  • Note any changes from normal

Catching problems early prevents costly emergencies. Most well failures show warning signs weeks or months before complete failure‚Äîif you're paying attention.
